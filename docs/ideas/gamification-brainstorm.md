# Gamification Ideas for Job Hunt Buddy

**Status:** Brainstorm (shelved for later consideration)
**Date:** 2025-01-26

## Goals

Make job hunting more encouraging, entertaining, and less frustrating through gamification.

### Target Feelings
- **Achievement & Progress** - "I'm making tangible progress even when I get rejections"
- **Motivation & Momentum** - "I want to keep going, even on tough days"
- **Fun & Levity** - "This makes job hunting feel less soul-crushing"

### Explicitly Avoided
- Community/Competition features - comparing to others can be detrimental to well-being

## Pain Points to Address

1. **The silence** - Applying and never hearing back feels like shouting into a void
2. **Rejections pile up** - Each "no" chips away at confidence
3. **The grind** - Repetitive tasks (tailoring resumes, writing cover letters) feel tedious
4. **Lack of visible progress** - Hard to see you're moving forward when nothing "lands"

## Approaches Explored

### Approach A: "Job Hunt Journey" (Visual Progress Focus)
A garden/landscape that grows as you progress. Each job you add plants a seed. Taking actions (applying, prepping, interviewing) nurtures the plants. Rejections don't kill plants - they add "experience compost" that helps future plants grow faster. The visual evolves from barren land → sprouts → garden → thriving ecosystem.

### Approach B: "XP & Levels" (Points Focus)
Earn XP for every action: adding jobs, writing cover letters, completing prep sessions, moving through pipeline stages. Level up to unlock cosmetic themes or encouraging titles ("Persistent Applicant" → "Interview Warrior" → "Offer Magnet"). Daily/weekly challenges for bonus XP.

### Approach C: "Energy & Momentum" (Anti-Burnout Focus)
Instead of rewarding more activity, this tracks sustainable effort. A "momentum meter" fills when you work consistently but warns if you're grinding too hard. Celebrates rest days. Reframes rejections as "redirections" with encouraging messages. Focuses on well-being over volume.

## Open Questions

- How prominent should gamification be? (Subtle vs. front-and-center)
- Which styles resonate most? (Leaning toward Visual Progress + XP)
- Should elements be combinable?

## Data Available for Gamification

The app already tracks:

- Resume grading scores
- Cover letters generated
- Prep Q&A sessions
- Notes, contacts, timeline events
- Skills extracted and tracked
- Career coach interactions
- Learning tasks
- Interview rounds
